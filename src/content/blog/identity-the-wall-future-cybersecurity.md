For decades, security meant building a wall and defending it. Inside was trusted;
outside was not. That model is over. Cloud, mobile, and remote work have
dissolved the perimeter, and identity has quietly become the new control plane.

This essay looks at what it means to treat identity — not the network edge — as
the primary boundary, and why the organizations that internalize this earliest
will have a structural security advantage.
