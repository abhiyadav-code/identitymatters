Privileged accounts are the keys to the kingdom — and attackers know it. Whether
it's a domain admin, a root credential, or a service account with sprawling
entitlements, privileged access is where the blast radius is largest and the
margin for error is smallest.

This piece walks through how Privileged Identity Management (PIM) and Privileged
Access Management (PAM) relate, where they overlap, and why a "set it and forget
it" mindset is exactly what adversaries are counting on.

## Why privileged access is different

Ordinary identity governance asks *who should have access to what*. Privileged
access raises the stakes: a single compromised admin credential can unwind every
other control you've invested in. The controls that matter most here are
just-in-time elevation, session monitoring, and credential vaulting.

## PIM vs. PAM

The two terms are often used interchangeably, but they answer different
questions — one about *which identities hold privilege over time*, the other
about *how privileged sessions are brokered and monitored in the moment*.
